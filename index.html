What is Satanism?

Satanism is a group of ideological and philosophical beliefs based on Satan. Contemporary religious practice of Satanism began with the founding of the Church of Satan in 1966, although a few historical precedents exist. Satanism is divided into different forms, including theistic Satanism (which sees Satan as a supernatural being) and atheistic Satanism (which views Satan as a symbol of human traits).

Theistic Satanists view Satan as an actual deity or supernatural being. They may worship Satan as a god, seeing him as a liberating and empowering figure who stands in opposition to the Christian God. Rituals and ceremonies often play a significant role, involving invocations, prayers, and offerings to Satan. Some practitioners may also incorporate elements of traditional occultism, such as magic and divination.

Atheistic Satanists do not believe in a literal Satan. Instead, they view Satan as a symbol of individualism, free will, and the rejection of arbitrary authority. Satan is seen as an archetype representing enlightenment and self-empowerment. Rather than worship, atheistic Satanists focus on self-development, self-expression, and living according to one's own principles. Rituals are often symbolic, meant to affirm personal beliefs rather than invoke supernatural forces.

What is Paganism?

Paganism is a term first used in the fourth century by early Christians for people in the Roman Empire who practiced polytheism or ethnic religions other than Judaism. Contemporary Paganism, also known as modern Paganism and Neopaganism, is a collective term for new religious movements influenced by or claiming to be derived from the various historical pagan beliefs of pre-modern Europe.

Modern Paganism includes diverse religious traditions such as Wicca, Druidry, Heathenry, and others. These traditions often focus on nature worship, polytheism, and the veneration of ancestors and spirits. Pagan practices can include rituals, festivals, magic, and the celebration of the cycles of nature, such as the solstices and equinoxes.
